## RESEARCH REPORT — Job Loss Due to AI — 2026-02-22

### Trending angles (what people are talking about right now)
- **"AI Washing"** — Sam Altman just admitted companies are FALSELY blaming AI for layoffs driven by restructuring/cost-cutting. Blowing up on Fortune, NYT, TOI (16 hours ago). Massive narrative shift.
- **Microsoft AI CEO (Mustafa Suleyman) says all white-collar work automated in 18 months** — massive fear-driver, viral on LinkedIn and X this week
- **India specifically ravaged** — Futurism piece on wave of suicides among India's IT workforce; entry-level displacement real and severe
- **The BLS Data Twist** — BLS survey shows no statistically significant change in unemployment for high-AI-exposure jobs post-ChatGPT launch. Reality vs. fear gap.
- **IBM tripling entry-level hiring** — counternarrative, 812 upvotes on r/cscareerquestions
- **WEF: 92 million roles displaced by 2030** — headline fear stat everyone's citing

### Viral hooks seen (actual lines working this week)
- "Companies are laying off workers because of AI's POTENTIAL — not its performance" (HBR)
- "AI was cited in 50,000+ layoffs in 2025" (NYT/Challenger Gray)
- "AI washing" — companies using AI as a PR shield for bad business decisions
- "Coding is dead. All that will be left are principal devs and product owners." (Reddit, viral)
- "$2.5 trillion in AI investment isn't creating wealth, it's creating unemployment" (5.6M views on X)
- "AI acts like a hungover college intern — with a $100K price tag" (Mark Cuban, Fortune)

### Content gaps (what nobody's covering)

- The India founder's POV: Speaking from inside India's AI wave (not from US media fear)
- What actually PROTECTS you vs. what doesn't (not generic "learn AI" advice)
- The entry-level vs. senior split — data shows entry-level hit hardest, not mid/senior
- Traditional ML as the layer that makes you irreplaceable (Ayush's core thesis applied to this pillar)
- The mental health angle — "I almost didn't want to live" + now watching peers panic — deeply personal content

- 21% of Asia jobs expected displaced by 2026 (manufacturing-heavy)
- WEF: 92 million roles by 2030
- BLS data: NO significant difference in unemployment rate for high-AI-exposure occupations vs. low-AI-exposure since ChatGPT launch
- Microsoft CEO: 18 months to automate all white-collar tasks
- IBM: tripling entry-level hiring in 2026
