## CROSS-PLATFORM PATTERN ANALYSIS

### What worked (by signal strength)

1. **Instagram "I did X before X was a thing" + insider authority + comment trigger**
   → VIRAL: 20K views, 1,200 comments, +700 followers
   → This format is the highest ROI play right now. Period.

2. **LinkedIn contrarian data post with specific numbers + CTA to resource**
   → "your company didn't lay you off because of AI" = 24 comments, 17 reposts
   → Structure: false consensus → real data → counter-narrative → p.s with resource link
   → The data (165K layoffs, BLS study, 50K citing AI) made it credible and shareable

3. **X thread (multi-tweet with numbered structure)**
   → Entry-level dying thread drove Feb 25 spike
   → Numbered format (1/11, 10/11, 11/11) creates completeness compulsion

### What didn't work

1. **LinkedIn satirical step-by-step (Jack Dorsey)**
   → 12,686 impressions, only 6 comments
   → Format: clever but didn't invite a response. People read, agreed, scrolled.
   → Missing: personal angle, a direct question, or a CTA that demands reaction

2. **LinkedIn observation post without a tension hook (India tech workforce)**
   → Highest impressions (18,265), lowest comments (1)
   → Good insight, but the hook ("something is happening...") was soft
   → No call to think or respond. People consumed it passively.

### Confirmed patterns

- **Comment trigger CTAs on Instagram = mandatory.** "Comment X" posts get 10-100x the engagement of passive CTA posts.
- **Reposts on LinkedIn = most reliable virality signal.** 17 reposts on post 3 = people found it shareable enough to put their name on it. That's the bar.
- **Impression volume ≠ resonance.** Post 2 had 18K impressions and 1 comment. Post 3 had 14K and 24 comments. Always optimize for comments + reposts over raw impressions.
- **Specific data > general observation.** The posts with BLS data, specific numbers (165K layoffs, 40% workforce cut) outperformed vague takes.
- **Personal authority framing beats news commentary.** "I worked on transformers before GPT existed" > "Here's what's happening with AI jobs."
