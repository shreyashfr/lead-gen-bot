Tweet 1:
How I'd rebuild our outreach system from scratch in 2026.

It looks nothing like what we were doing 18 months ago. 🧵

---

Tweet 2:
The old way: hire 2-3 SDRs. Spend weeks writing sequences. A/B test subject lines for months. Wait 60 days. Hope the pipeline numbers are worth it.

Most of that time isn't selling. It's setup, guessing, and waiting.

---

Tweet 3:
SDRs spend 40-60% of their time on tasks that don't need a human. Writing follow-ups. Updating CRMs. Chasing replies that never come.

The bottleneck isn't headcount. It's manual work dressed up as sales work.

---

Tweet 4:
The new approach: Vibe Campaigns.

You describe who you want to reach and what you want to say. The system builds the campaign and runs it. No sequence builder. No manual follow-up logic.

---

Tweet 5:
The brief isn't complicated. Something like: "Target SaaS founders in the US, 10-50 employees. Angle: slow follow-up is killing their pipeline. Offer: show them how we fixed that."

That's your campaign prompt. That's all you need to start.

---

Tweet 6:
Sbl.so takes that brief and handles the rest. Connection requests, opening messages, follow-ups, conversation management when someone replies.

No manual sequencing. No hand-written "just following up" messages at 9pm.

---

Tweet 7:
What still needs a human: the ICP decision. Someone has to define who actually matters, and why.

And the close. When a prospect is warm and ready to talk, a human takes it from there. The system's job is getting them to that point.

---

Tweet 8:
The messaging isn't generic either. The sequencing is built around real persuasion principles from the Godfather of Persuasion.

Most tools ignore this entirely. It's one reason the response rates don't look the same.

---

Tweet 9:
The playbook: write the brief, run the campaign, handle the warm conversations.

That's a full outreach motion with one person managing it. Sbl.so runs the rest. Drop a reply if you want to see what a brief actually looks like.
