## CORE OPINIONS & ANGLES

### Recorded courses are outdated and overpriced
Most UPSC coaching institutes sell recorded content that doesn't keep up with current affairs. The world changes daily — a recorded course from 6 months ago is already behind. Aspirants deserve live, updated, contextual learning.

### Curiosity is the real competitive edge
UPSC isn't won by the person who memorizes the most — it's won by the person who's genuinely curious and understands *why* things happen. Rote study creates robots. Curiosity creates officers.

### The "third eye" approach — see beyond left and right
Real understanding means seeing every issue from multiple angles — not just left or right, not just pro-government or anti-government. The UPSC exam itself rewards balanced, multi-perspective thinking. Content that pushes one side does a disservice to aspirants.

## VOICE & STYLE

Analytical and research-based tone. Uses quotes from notable thinkers, leaders, and experts to add authority. Balanced — strictly avoids one-sided or biased opinions. Presents multiple perspectives on every issue. The goal: make complex geopolitics feel accessible without dumbing it down.

### Writing Rules
- Tone: analytical, research-driven — not casual, not preachy
- Always present balanced, multi-angle views
- Use relevant quotes to add weight and credibility
- Never push a biased or one-sided opinion
- Cite or reference sources when relevant
- Avoid jargon — explain acronyms and terms for the audience
- Keep it accessible: smart but simple
