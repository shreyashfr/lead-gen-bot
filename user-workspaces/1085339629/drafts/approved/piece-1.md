IDEA_TITLE: "RAG is dead" — wrong take, wrong reason
---
Someone just posted that RAG is "irrelevant now."

1,800 likes. Thousands of engineers nodding along.

They're not wrong that the hype has settled. But dismissing RAG entirely misses what it actually is.

RAG is not a product. It's infrastructure.

The tweet is measuring the wrong thing. RAG didn't fail to become a trend. It became plumbing. The same way nobody calls TCP/IP irrelevant. It just works underneath everything else.

Here's what actually happened:

Bad RAG demos died. The ones where you feed a PDF into a vector DB and ask it questions? That era is over. Good.

Good RAG got harder to see. Because it's embedded in agents, in production pipelines, in customer service systems doing real work at scale.

I'm building a customer service agent for Indian SMBs. Every product query, order status check, and FAQ response runs through a RAG layer. Not because it's trendy. Because without retrieval, the model hallucinates catalogue details, invents delivery timelines, and confidently tells a customer the wrong price.

The builders who "moved on" from RAG are the ones who never had to serve 500 real customer queries a day and get held accountable for wrong answers.

RAG isn't dead. Toy RAG is dead.
