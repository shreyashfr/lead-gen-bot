# LinkedIn Post Format Reference

## Structure
```
[HOOK LINE — one sentence, stops the scroll]

[1-2 lines of context or story setup]

[Body — 3-5 short paragraphs, each 1-3 sentences]

[Landing line — sharp, no call to action]
```

## Hook Patterns That Work For {USER_NAME}
- Specific number + bold claim: "I got my first job in AI/ML on a 4GB RAM PC."
- Contrarian opener: "Traditional ML is dead. Bullshit."
- Embarrassing admission: "I thought 3 lines of Scikit-Learn made me an ML engineer."
- Result → reverse: "I run an AI company. In 2025 I didn't want to be alive."
- Direct address: "If you're filling surveys online right now — I've been there."

## Good Example
```
I got my first job in AI/ML on a 4GB RAM PC.

Not a MacBook. Not a gaming rig. A basic PC that took 3 minutes to open Chrome.

My family had just lost almost everything. Dad lost big money in the stock market. Relatives stopped talking to us. Mom was crying.

I had two options. Wait until things got better. Or open the laptop I had and start.

I started.

Every week students tell me: "I need a better laptop first." "I'll start once I get a GPU." "My PC can't run these models."

No. That's not the problem.

I ran models that took 20 minutes to train on that machine. Got a $600/week AI job before I turned 18.

You don't start with your best setup. You start with what you have.

The laptop was never the bottleneck. You are.
```

## Bad Patterns to Avoid
- Starting with "I am excited to share..."
- Ending with "Drop a comment below if you agree!"
- Bullet lists inside the post body
- Vague statements without specific numbers or moments
- Em dashes, semicolons
- Headers inside the post
