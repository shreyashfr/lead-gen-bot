# Self-Check — Run Before Every Output

This is the final validation pass. Run through every check below before delivering ANY written content. No exceptions.

---

## CHECK 1: Banned Word Scan

Search the output for these words. If found, rewrite the sentence.

**Instant fail (rewrite immediately):**
delve, tapestry (figurative), testament (figurative), landscape (figurative), pivotal, intricate, meticulous, underscore (as verb), vibrant, foster/fostering, garner/garnered, bolster/bolstered, interplay, showcase/showcasing, nestled, groundbreaking, renowned, indelible, encompasses, multifaceted, paradigm, synergy

**Cluster check:** If ANY two words from the contextual ban list (Additionally, enhance, align, highlighting, emphasizing, key, valuable, comprehensive, innovative, robust, seamless, dynamic, holistic) appear in the same paragraph, rewrite one of them.

---

## CHECK 2: Em Dash Count

Count all em dashes (—) in the entire piece.

- 0-1: Good
- 2: Acceptable, but review if both are in the same paragraph
- 3+: Rewrite. Replace with commas, colons, parentheses, or periods.

---

## CHECK 3: The -ing Analysis Tail

Search for sentences ending with:
- ", highlighting..."
- ", emphasizing..."
- ", underscoring..."
- ", showcasing..."
- ", reflecting..."
- ", contributing to..."
- ", fostering..."
- ", marking..."
- ", symbolizing..."
- ", ensuring..."

If found: delete the -ing clause or rewrite as a separate specific sentence.

---

## CHECK 4: Rule of Three

Count instances of "X, Y, and Z" pattern (three parallel items in a list).

- 0-1 per piece: Fine
- 2: Review — can you break one into two items or four?
- 3+: Rewrite. This is a major AI tell.

---

## CHECK 5: Copulative Avoidance

Search for:
- "serves as" → replace with "is"
- "stands as" → replace with "is"
- "boasts" (meaning "has") → replace with "has"
- "features" (meaning "has") → replace with "has" or "includes"
- "offers" (in descriptive, non-product context) → replace with "has" or "gives"
- "holds the distinction of being" → replace with "is"
- "represents" (when "is" works) → replace with "is"

Count how many times "is" and "are" appear. If they're suspiciously absent in a 500+ word piece, add them back.

---

## CHECK 6: Paragraph Starters

List the first word of each paragraph. Look for:

- 3+ paragraphs starting with "The" → vary openers
- Any paragraph starting with "Additionally" or "Furthermore" or "Moreover" → rewrite the opener
- Consecutive paragraphs starting with the same word → fix

---

## CHECK 7: Last Paragraph Test

Read the final paragraph. Does it:

- Start with "In summary", "In conclusion", "Overall", "To summarize"? → Delete or rewrite with new information
- Restate what the first paragraph said? → Delete or rewrite
- End with "...setting the stage for..." or "...as they continue to..."? → Rewrite
- Contain a vague positive outlook about the future? → Delete

The last paragraph should either add new information, state a specific next step, or end cleanly on the last substantial point.

---

## CHECK 8: Significance Inflation

Search for phrases like:
- "reflects broader trends"
- "marks a shift"
- "is a reminder"
- "underscores the importance"
- "highlights the significance"
- "represents a turning point"
- "contributes to the broader"
- "shaping the future of"
- "the evolving [anything]"

If found: delete the entire clause or sentence. If the point is important, the facts will show it without these phrases.

---

## CHECK 9: Sentence Length Variance

Sample 10 consecutive sentences. Count the words in each.

- If all are between 15-25 words: add some 5-8 word sentences and some 30+ word sentences
- If standard deviation is low (all similar length): vary it
- If no sentence is under 8 words in a 500+ word piece: add some short punchy ones

---

## CHECK 10: The Read-Aloud Test

Read each paragraph out loud (mentally). Flag anything that sounds like:

- A press release → rewrite to be conversational
- A travel brochure → cut the flowery descriptors
- A college admissions essay → make it direct
- A LinkedIn motivational post → remove the performative sincerity
- A product ad → cut the puffery, keep the specifics
- A Wikipedia article about significance → cut the meta-commentary

---

## CHECK 11: Formatting Tells

- **Headings:** Are they in sentence case? ("How we did it" not "How We Did It")
- **Bold:** More than 2 bold items per section? Reduce.
- **Bullet lists:** Any bullets with "**Bold Header:** description" format? Rewrite as prose or plain bullets.
- **Emoji:** Any emoji in professional content? Remove (unless casual Slack/WhatsApp context).
- **Quotes:** Are they straight ("") or curly ("")? Must be straight.
- **Tables:** Any table with fewer than 4 rows? Convert to prose.

---

## CHECK 12: Ghost Phrases

Search for these chatbot artifacts that sometimes leak through:

- "It's important to note"
- "It's worth mentioning"
- "It bears noting"
- "In today's [noun] landscape"
- "In an era of"
- "Let's dive in" / "Let's delve into"
- "At its core"
- "When it comes to"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Moving forward"
- "Take it to the next level"
- "Game-changer"
- "Best-in-class"
- "World-class"
- "State-of-the-art"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "End-to-end"
- "One-stop shop"
- "Seamless integration"
- "Robust solution"
- "Rich ecosystem"

If found: rewrite with specific language or delete.

---

## CHECK 13: Voice Match

Ask: "Would Ayush actually say this?"

Quick tests:
- Is there a sentence longer than 40 words that could be split? → Split it.
- Is there a paragraph without any specific number, name, or concrete detail? → Add specificity or cut the paragraph.
- Is there any sentence that compliments the reader or subject without adding information? → Cut it.
- Does the opening sentence get to the point in the first line? → If not, move the point up.
- Are there any "would you like" or "feel free to" endings? → Delete them.

---

## CHECK 14: The Challenges Pattern

Search for any variation of:
1. "Despite its [positive], [subject] faces challenges"
2. followed by generic challenge list
3. followed by "Despite these challenges, [subject] continues to..."

If this pattern exists in any form: completely restructure the section. Discuss challenges naturally within relevant context, not as a formulaic bookend.

---

## CHECK 15: Elegant Variation

Check if the same entity is referred to by 3+ different names/synonyms within a short span:
- "the company... the firm... the organization... the enterprise"
- "the artist... the creator... the visionary... the innovator"

If found: pick one term (or use pronouns) and stick with it. Repetition is human. Synonym cycling is AI.

---

## FINAL PASS

After all checks pass, read the entire piece one more time with this single question:

**"If someone ran this through GPTZero, Originality.ai, or had a heavy LLM user review it — would any paragraph raise a flag?"**

If yes, identify which paragraph and rewrite it. If no, ship it.
